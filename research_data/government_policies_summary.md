This research document compiles official government renewable energy policies, targets, and incentives from 15 countries across Africa, Middle East, Asia-Pacific, and Latin America. The data focuses on renewable energy targets for 2030 and 2050, feed-in tariff mechanisms, carbon pricing, investment incentives, and regulatory frameworks.

## Key Findings

### 1. Renewable Energy Targets

**Most Ambitious 2030 Targets:**
- **Kenya**: 100% clean energy on national grid
- **Ethiopia**: 95% renewable electricity
- **Australia**: 82% renewable energy
- **Chile**: 80% renewable energy (legally binding)

**Capacity Expansion Leaders:**
- **India**: 500 GW renewable capacity target
- **Saudi Arabia**: 130 GW capacity target
- **South Africa**: 42 GW through REIPPPP program

### 2. Feed-in Tariff Trends

**Key Observation:** Major shift from feed-in tariffs to competitive auction systems.

**Countries with Active Feed-in Tariffs:**
- **Egypt**: Solar $0.084/kWh, Wind $0.04/kWh
- **Saudi Arabia**: $0.0187/kWh government purchase price

**Countries That Phased Out FiTs:**
- Morocco, Kenya, Jordan, Vietnam, South Africa
- All transitioned to competitive bidding/auction systems

**Auction-Based Pricing (2024):**
- **India Solar**: ₹2.50-2.68/kWh ($0.030-0.032/kWh)
- **India Wind**: ₹3.42-3.81/kWh ($0.041-0.046/kWh)
- **UAE Solar**: $0.0135/kWh (world-class competitive pricing)

### 3. Carbon Pricing Mechanisms

**Countries with Active Carbon Pricing (9/15):**

**Voluntary Carbon Markets:**
- **Egypt**: Africa's first regulated VCM on stock exchange
- **Morocco**: Article 6 mechanisms with Switzerland and Norway
- **Saudi Arabia**: Regional VCM Company (RVCMC) with successful auctions
- **UAE**: Carbon Trading Exchange and Clearing House since 2021

**Carbon Tax:**
- **Chile**: Increasing to $35/tCO₂ by 2030, $80/tCO₂ by 2040

**Emissions Trading Systems:**
- **Indonesia**: ETS covering 99 coal plants, IDXCarbon launched 2023
- **Australia**: Australian Carbon Credit Units (ACCUs) system

### 4. Investment Incentives

**Major Financial Incentives by Country:**

**India ($2.57 billion):**
- Production Linked Incentive for solar manufacturing
- Waiver of transmission charges
- Accelerated depreciation

**Australia (Multiple billions):**
- $6.7B Hydrogen Production Tax Incentive
- $20B Rewiring the Nation (grid investment)
- $7.1B Critical Minerals Production Tax Incentive

**UAE ($54 billion):**
- Dubai Green Fund: $27 billion for clean energy loans
- Major solar park investments

**Mexico ($48 billion):**
- Investment plan for 51 renewable projects by 2030

**Indonesia ($20 billion):**
- Just Energy Transition Partnership (JETP) funding

**South Africa ($20 billion):**
- REIPPPP program attracted over $20B in private investment

### 5. Local Content Requirements

**Documented Requirements:**
- **Morocco**: 30% LCR for Solar PV and CSP projects (achieved 42% in Noor I project)
- **India**: Encouraged through customs duties (25% on solar cells, 40% on modules)
- **South Africa**: Evaluated as REIPPPP bid criteria

**Notable:** Most countries lack explicit local content mandates, focusing instead on attracting foreign investment.

### 6. Cost Reduction Trends

**Significant Price Declines:**
- **South Africa REIPPPP**: 55% reduction in wind costs, 76% reduction in solar PV costs
- **UAE**: Achieved $0.0135/kWh solar through 15+ years of R&D
- **India**: Solar tariffs stable at ₹2.5-2.68/kWh, highly competitive

### 7. Policy Mechanisms Evolution

**Transition Pattern Observed:**
1. **Phase 1 (2008-2015)**: Feed-in tariffs to kickstart markets (Kenya 2008, Vietnam 2011, Egypt 2014)
2. **Phase 2 (2015-2020)**: FiT refinement and early auctions
3. **Phase 3 (2020-present)**: Competitive auctions replace FiTs for price discovery

**Countries Still Using FiTs:**
- Egypt (limited rounds)
- Saudi Arabia (transitioning)
- Ethiopia (PPP framework)

### 8. Grid Connection Policies

**Priority Access Models:**
- Kenya: Guaranteed connection rights for qualifying projects
- Egypt: Priority grid connection for renewable projects
- Indonesia: Priority dispatch for renewables

**Transmission Investment:**
- Australia: $20B Rewiring the Nation program
- India: Waiver of Inter-State Transmission Charges
- Vietnam: Grid expansion under Power Development Plan 8

### 9. Legal and Regulatory Frameworks

**Legally Binding Targets:**
- **Chile**: Framework Law on Climate Change (2022)
- **Australia**: Legislated emissions reduction targets

**Comprehensive Policy Frameworks:**
- **Morocco**: Laws 57-09, 37-16, 13-09, 48-15
- **Kenya**: Energy Act 2019, REAP policy
- **Ethiopia**: Energy Proclamation No. 810/2013, CRGE strategy
- **Brazil**: Laws 14,993/2024, 15,097/2025, 14,300/2022

### 10. Challenges and Risks

**Implementation Gaps:**
- **Indonesia**: Only 14.68% renewable despite 44% target for 2030
- **Mexico**: Lack of detailed financing mechanisms
- **Egypt**: Only 11% renewable vs 42% target for 2030

**Infrastructure Constraints:**
- **Chile**: 19% renewables curtailment due to transmission issues
- **Kenya**: Need for rapid capacity additions

**Political Risks:**
- **Mexico**: Previous administration prioritized fossil fuels
- **Indonesia**: Competing interests with coal industry
