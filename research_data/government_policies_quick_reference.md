## Investment Risk Assessment

### Low Risk (Bankable)
- **Morocco**: Proven track record, MASEN support, 30% local content enables partnerships
- **Chile**: Legally binding targets, mature market
- **Australia**: Government-backed incentives, stable regulatory environment
- **South Africa**: REIPPPP demonstrated bankability

### Medium Risk (Growing Markets)
- **India**: Scale opportunity, competitive auctions, PLI incentives
- **Kenya**: On track to exceed targets, proven REIPPPP model
- **UAE**: Strong government support, competitive pricing

### Higher Risk (Implementation Challenges)

- **Indonesia**: Only 14.68% renewable vs 44% target
- **Mexico**: Lack of detailed financing, political uncertainty
- **Saudi Arabia**: Early stage, rapid transition needed

## Key Takeaways for Investors

### ✓ Global Trend: FiT → Competitive Auctions
Feed-in tariffs being phased out globally in favor of competitive bidding for price discovery and cost reduction.

### ✓ Carbon Markets Growing
9 of 15 countries have active carbon pricing, primarily voluntary carbon markets in MENA and developing countries.

### ✓ Major Incentive Programs
Multiple billion-dollar programs in Australia, UAE, India, Indonesia provide investment opportunities.

### ✓ Local Content Variability
Morocco has explicit 30% requirement; India encourages through tariffs; most others lack mandates.

### ✓ Legal Certainty
Chile and Australia have legally binding targets, reducing policy risk.

### ✓ Grid Investment Critical
Countries investing heavily in transmission (Australia $20B, India waivers) show commitment to targets.

### ✓ Implementation Gaps
Monitor countries with large gaps between targets and current status (Egypt, Indonesia, Mexico).
