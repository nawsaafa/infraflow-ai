## Executive Summary

This research compiled comprehensive country risk data for 70 countries across three target regions: Middle East & North Africa (17 countries), Sub-Saharan Africa (30 countries), and South America/Latin America (23 countries). Data was collected from official sources including OECD, Transparency International, major credit rating agencies (Moody's, S&P, Fitch), and the World Bank.

### Key Findings:

1. **Regional Risk Distribution:**
   - **Lowest Risk:** Middle East Gulf states (UAE, Qatar, Kuwait) with OECD categories 1-2
   - **Moderate Risk:** Chile, Uruguay, and Botswana leading their respective regions
   - **Highest Risk:** 18 countries in OECD category 7, including Argentina, Venezuela, Nigeria, and conflict-affected states

2. **Corruption Levels:**
   - **Best Performers:** Uruguay (76), UAE (68), Chile (63), Saudi Arabia (59), Qatar (59)
   - **Worst Performers:** South Sudan (8), Somalia (9), Venezuela (10), Syria (12), Yemen (13)
   - Regional average for MENA: 39/100 (below global average of 43)

3. **Credit Ratings:**
   - **Investment Grade:** 11 countries (mostly Gulf states, Chile, Uruguay, Botswana)
   - **Speculative Grade:** 47 countries
   - **Default/Selective Default:** 7 countries (Ghana, Ethiopia, Lebanon, Zambia, Venezuela, others)

## Data Sources

### Primary Official Sources:

1. **[OECD Country Risk Classifications](https://www.oecd.org/en/topics/country-risk-classification.html)**
   - Classification scale: 0 (lowest risk) to 7 (highest risk)
   - Updated multiple times annually by Country Risk Expert (CRE) group
   - Reflects likelihood of external debt repayment

2. **[Transparency International - Corruption Perceptions Index 2024](https://www.transparency.org/en/cpi/2024)**
   - Scale: 0 (highly corrupt) to 100 (very clean)
   - Covers 180 countries worldwide
   - Published February 2025

3. **Sovereign Credit Ratings (2024):**
   - [Moody's Sovereign Ratings](https://www.moodys.com/)
   - [S&P Global Ratings](https://www.spglobal.com/ratings/)
   - [Fitch Ratings](https://www.fitchratings.com/)

4. **[World Bank Doing Business 2020](https://archive.doingbusiness.org/)** (Archived)
   - Last edition before discontinuation in 2021
   - Rankings for 190 economies
   - Data integrity concerns led to discontinuation

5. **[World Bank Worldwide Governance Indicators 2024](https://www.worldbank.org/en/publication/worldwide-governance-indicators)**
   - Six governance dimensions (1996-2023 data)
   - Political Stability, Regulatory Quality, Rule of Law, etc.
   - Note: Detailed country-level WGI scores require direct database access

## Regional Analysis

### Middle East & North Africa (17 countries)

#### Top Performers:
- **UAE (OECD Cat 1, CPI 68, Aa2/AA/AA-):** Regional leader in governance and investment climate
- **Qatar (OECD Cat 2, CPI 59, Aa2/AA-/AA):** Strong fiscal position, upgraded by Fitch in 2024
- **Kuwait (OECD Cat 2, CPI 46, A1/A+/AA-):** Solid sovereign ratings despite political challenges

#### Notable Developments:
- Saudi Arabia upgraded to Aa3 by Moody's (November 2024) - first upgrade since 2016
- Oman recovered investment-grade status (Baa3/BBB-), CPI improved by 12 points to 55
- Four Arab countries (Saudi Arabia, Jordan, Bahrain, Kuwait) among world's top 10 business climate improvers in DB 2020
- Lebanon saw largest decline in CPI, down 6 points from 28 (2019) to 22 (2024)

#### Challenges:
- Conflict-affected states (Syria, Yemen, Libya) have lowest ratings
- Regional CPI average increased only 1 point in 12 years to 39/100
- Iraq, despite improvements (+3 CPI points), remains in OECD category 7

### Sub-Saharan Africa (30 countries)

#### Top Performers:
- **Botswana (OECD Cat 3, CPI 57, A3/BBB+/A-):** Highest-rated African sovereign
- **Rwanda (OECD Cat 5, CPI 57, B2/B+/B+):** Top CPI score in region
- **South Africa (OECD Cat 4, CPI 41, Ba2/BB-/BB-):** S&P revised outlook to positive in 2024

#### Notable Developments:
- Côte d'Ivoire upgraded from Ba3 to Ba2 by Moody's
- Nigeria upgraded from Caa1 to B3 by Moody's (May 2025) following reforms
- Tanzania improved to B1 from B2 by Moody's
- Kenya, Benin, and Côte d'Ivoire re-entered Eurobond market in 2024 after nearly two years
- Togo identified as "bright spot" in reform efforts

#### Challenges:
- Only 2 economies rank in top 50 on Doing Business ease of doing business
- 12 of bottom 20 Doing Business rankings from Sub-Saharan Africa
- Default situations: Ghana (SD), Ethiopia (SD), Zambia (SD)
- South Sudan (CPI 8) and Somalia (CPI 9) lowest globally

### South America & Latin America (23 countries)

#### Top Performers:
- **Chile (OECD Cat 3, CPI 63, A1/A/A-):** Highest rated in region
- **Uruguay (OECD Cat 3, CPI 76, Baa2/BBB/BBB-):** Highest CPI in South America, strong institutions
- **Peru (OECD Cat 3, CPI 31, Baa1/BBB-/BBB):** Investment grade despite downgrade in 2024

#### Notable Developments:
- Paraguay upgraded to OECD category 4 (October 2024)
- Costa Rica upgraded to OECD category 3 (October 2024)
- Argentina rating raised from CCC- to CCC by S&P (March 2024) but remains vulnerable to default
- Peru downgraded from BBB to BBB- by S&P (April 2024)
- Colombia's S&P outlook revised to negative (January 2024) due to weak investor confidence
- Highest number of positive outlooks since 2011 in Americas region

#### Challenges:
- No Latin American economy ranks in top 50 on Doing Business
- Mexico (rank 60) remains region's top-ranked in Doing Business
- Venezuela in default (CPI 10, third lowest globally)
- Argentina chronically vulnerable to default despite recent reforms
- Average regional rating around BB

## Data Quality & Limitations

### Complete Data:
- OECD Country Risk Classifications (partial - many countries not included)
- Corruption Perceptions Index 2024 (complete for all 70 countries)
- Sovereign Credit Ratings from major agencies (mostly complete)

### Limited/Unavailable Data:
- **World Bank Worldwide Governance Indicators:** Detailed scores for Political Stability and Regulatory Quality require direct database access at [databank.worldbank.org](https://databank.worldbank.org/source/worldwide-governance-indicators)
- **Doing Business Rankings:** Discontinued in 2021; only 2020 data available
- **OECD Categories:** Not all countries classified (high-income OECD members exempt, some countries not covered)

### Data Integrity Notes:
1. World Bank Doing Business was discontinued due to data irregularities discovered in 2018 and 2020 reports
2. OECD classifications updated multiple times per year - data reflects October 2024 / early 2025 updates
3. Some countries (war-torn, failed states) not rated by major credit agencies
4. Sovereign ratings represent point-in-time assessments and can change rapidly

## Technical Notes

### OECD Country Risk Categories:
- **0-1:** Negligible risk
- **2-3:** Low to moderate risk
- **4-5:** Moderate to elevated risk
- **6-7:** High to very high risk

### Credit Rating Scale (Investment Grade):
- **Moody's:** Aaa to Baa3
- **S&P/Fitch:** AAA to BBB-

### Credit Rating Scale (Speculative Grade):
- **Moody's:** Ba1 to C
- **S&P/Fitch:** BB+ to D

### Corruption Perceptions Index Interpretation:
- **70-100:** Very clean, low corruption
- **50-69:** Clean, moderate corruption
- **30-49:** Corrupt, significant issues
- **10-29:** Very corrupt, pervasive corruption
- **0-9:** Extremely corrupt, failed governance
